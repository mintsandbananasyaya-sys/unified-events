## Scoring System

Unified Events does not operate on a formal numerical scoring system or maintain a persistent ranked tier/rating system for individual players. Seasonal outcomes are instead determined through end-of-season resolution, evaluating survival, territorial control, diplomatic standing, and recorded achievements.

## Tiers

The player search page pulls tier data from two external sites:
• **MCTiers** (mctiers.com) — covers SMP, UHC, Mace, Sword, Axe, NethPot
• **PvPTiers** (pvptiers.com) — covers Mace, Pot, Axe, Sword, UHC, SMP, NethPot
The player's **best tier** across both sites is shown for each mode. Tier ranking from worst to best: LT5 → HT5 → LT4 → HT4 → LT3 → HT3 → LT2 → HT2 → LT1 → HT1.

## What is Ht1

Tier ranking from worst to best: **LT5 → HT5 → LT4 → HT4 → LT3 → HT3 → LT2 → HT2 → LT1 → HT1**.
LT = Low Tier, HT = High Tier. HT1 is the highest achievable rank, LT5 is the lowest. The number represents the tier level (1 being best, 5 being lowest).

## my Tiers

Your tiers are visible on the player search page at https://unified-events.onrender.com/players.html — search your own username or IGN. Your tiers are pulled automatically from MCTiers and PvPTiers based on your linked IGN. Make sure you've run **/setign** first so your IGN is on record.

## no Tier Data

If no tier data is showing for a player, it means their IGN isn't registered on MCTiers or PvPTiers, or they haven't linked their IGN with **/setign**. Tiers are only pulled if a Minecraft IGN is on file. Bedrock players may also not have tier data if they haven't played on Java-based competitive servers.

## dm Not Working

If the bot can't DM you, your Discord privacy settings might be blocking DMs from server members. Go to **User Settings → Privacy & Safety** and enable 'Allow direct messages from server members'. Alternatively, allow DMs from this specific server in the server's privacy settings.
